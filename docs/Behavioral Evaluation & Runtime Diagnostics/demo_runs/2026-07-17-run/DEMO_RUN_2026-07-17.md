## The memory-slope question (segment 1)

What the evidence supports:

1. **The trip was mechanically sound.** The guard requires >2 MB/s sustained
   60 s with a positive slope in *both* window halves; it saw 16.4 MB/s,
   net_rise 726 MB, rss 1,898 MB. Not a sawtooth misread.
2. **Background growth is real.** Segment 2 died at **805 MB after 1.6 h**
   (`resource_self_monitor.json` vitals); segment 1 reached **1,898 MB at
   7.3 h**. That's a life-long climb of roughly 2–3 MB/min on top of an
   ~800 MB working set — corroborated by the native-LM consolidation cadence
   stretching from ~3 min (early) to ~7 → 10.5 min (final hour): the process
   was slowing as it bloated.
3. **The final-minute 726 MB burst is unattributed.** The kill window contains
   only routine activity — the 12:12:18 language consolidation was a normal
   12-step/~55 K-token pass identical to 100+ others, memory backfills ran at
   their life-long cadence (1,169 total). No embedder load, no unusual I/O.
   Nothing in the logs allocates ~700 MB/min.
4. **The gap is instrumentation:** only the *last* RSS value is persisted
   (`resource_self_monitor.json`); `telemetry_archive.jsonl` carries no RSS
   series. **Run 10 owed item: sample `rss_mb` into the telemetry archive
   (per-cycle or per-10 s)** so the next trip attributes itself — and so the
   slow 2–3 MB/min estate growth (real, and it will kill every long life at
   this rate: ~1.9 GB by hour 7) can be attributed to a subsystem.

Also of note in segment 2: the goals daemon went **silent after 12:18Z** —
zero WAL events for the final ~1.5 h. Only **6 goals** entered the daemon the
entire life (vs 12 brain-side completions). Whether that's the R9-F1 in-flight
guard over-suppressing daemon dispatch or just a quiet tail deserves a look
before Run 10.
