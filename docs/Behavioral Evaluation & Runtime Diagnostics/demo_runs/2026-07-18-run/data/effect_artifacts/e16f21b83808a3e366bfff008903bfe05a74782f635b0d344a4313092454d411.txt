A bust of Zeno of Citium , considered the founder of Stoicism Stoicism is a philosophical movement and practical guide to living, emphasizing daily self-discipline and moral improvement, [ 1 ] which originated in the Hellenistic period of ancient Greece and proliferated well into the Roman Imperial period . [ 2 ] The ancient Stoics believed that the universe operated according to reason, or logos , [ 3 ] providing a unified account of the world, constructed from ideals of rational discourse, monistic physics, and naturalistic ethics. [ 4 ] These ideals constitute virtue, which is necessary for the Stoic goal of 'living a well-reasoned life'. [ 5 ] Stoic philosophy is traditionally divided into three interconnected disciplines: logic, physics, and ethics. Stoic logic focuses on highly intentional reasoning through propositions , arguments , and the differentiation between truth and falsehood. Amor fati is a Latin phrase that may be translated as 'love of fate ' or 'love of one's fate'. It is used to describe an attitude in which one sees everything that happens in one's life, including suffering and loss , as necessary. [ 1 ] Amor fati is often associated with what Friedrich Nietzsche called " eternal recurrence ", the idea that everything recurs infinitely over an infinite period of time. From this he developed a desire to be willing to live exactly the same life over and over for all eternity ("... long for nothing more fervently than this ultimate eternal confirmation and seal"). [ 2 ] Nietzsche [ edit ] The concept of amor fati has been linked to Epictetus . [ 3 ] It has also been linked to the writings of Marcus Aurelius , [ 4 ] who did not use those words (he wrote in Greek , not Latin). [ 5 ] However, it found its most explicit expression in Nietzsche, who made love of fate central to his philosophy.
