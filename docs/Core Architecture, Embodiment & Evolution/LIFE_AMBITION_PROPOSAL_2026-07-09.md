## 1. The idea in one paragraph

People do not have end goals; they have **end-goal beliefs** — concrete,
revisable convictions about what their life is building toward, minted from
their values, their story so far, and the fact that they will die. The belief
does real work: it compresses directional values into one steerable imagined
destination, gives sacrifices a justification, and gives the autobiography an
arc. Orrin's aspirations are correctly unfinishable (directions, not
destinations — keep that). What's missing is the organ that lets him *author
and hold a believed destination* on top of them. Every ingredient already
exists in the runtime; nothing composes them.

## 3. Design — `brain/cognition/self_state/life_ambition.py`

One small module, four responsibilities. Symbolic-only; no LLM anywhere.

**3a. Authoring (once per life, past a maturity gate).** After the first
narrative update (not at birth — an ambition needs a story to grow out of),
compose ONE life-scoped ambition from: the aspiration scoreboard (prefer the
*starved* aspiration — this makes the ambition a counterweight to monopoly,
never an amplifier), recurring memory themes, and `days_remaining_felt` for
scale. The ambition MUST be a destination: a checkable done-condition, not a
direction. Template-composed, e.g. *"Before this life ends: produce N connected
artifacts on <theme> that each pass the quality predicate and cite a prior
one."* Done-criteria are machine-checkable against the effect ledger + quality
standard — no self-graded arrival.

**3b. Holding.** Persist as `data/life_ambition.json`, opinion-shaped:
`statement`, `done_criteria` (structured, checkable), `serves` (aspiration
drive), `confidence`/`alpha`/`beta`, `formed_at`, `revisions[]`. Surface it in
two existing mouths: the autobiography (`narrative_update` gains a one-line
prospective clause — "what I'm building toward") and `autogenerated_thoughts`
(the days-remaining thought can now bind remaining time to a specific intended
arrival).

**3c. Revising and feeding.** Progress against `done_criteria` (checked cheaply
on goal completion via the effect ledger) feeds `alpha`; sustained stagnation
feeds `beta`. Revision is allowed but expensive: only at narrative-pressure
peaks, at most every K cycles, and a revision appends to `revisions[]` — the
old ambition is history, never erased. This is the arrival-fallacy mechanic:
he can reach it and mint a new one, or lose faith and re-author.

**3d. Dying.** At termination, the final audit computes the verdict —
**arrived / died trying / abandoned (revised away)** — and the life capsule
seals the ambition history + verdict. The next life does NOT inherit the
ambition (it isn't his); it may inherit the *question* as a seed memory:
"the previous life aimed at X and <verdict>."

**Homing the will (the Finding-3 fix, folded in).** `driven_by="will"` goals
credit toward the current ambition's `serves` aspiration when their content
matches the ambition theme; general otherwise per the T0.3 alias table. His
volition gets an address. **Prerequisite:** identify where `driven_by="will"`
credit currently orphans (`drive_aspiration_credit` in `brain/data_schema.py` /
`brain/agency/effect_ledger.py` / `brain/cognition/planning/commitment_value.py`)
before wiring this.

## 4. Guardrails (so this doesn't recreate Run 5)

- **No second monopoly.** The ambition may add a small commitment bias
  (suggest ≤ 0.1, additive) to goals matching its theme, hard-capped by the
  existing aspiration-share quotas (`_cap_candidate_aspiration_share`). It
  biases, never dictates. **This is the part that must wait for Run 8 G1.**
- **Starved-aspiration preference at authoring** — the ambition should pull
  *against* whatever the scoreboard shows dominating, by construction.
- **Destination discipline.** Done-criteria must be ledger-checkable. If the
  authoring pass cannot produce checkable criteria, no ambition is formed that
  cycle. A direction wearing a goal costume is worse than nothing.
- **Orthogonality.** Land after the monopoly is solved (Run 8 G1 green), in its
  own commit, so it can't confound S9/S10.

## 6. Phases

- **P1 — represent + author** (`life_ambition.py`, `life_ambition.json`,
  authoring template, maturity gate). Unit-testable without a run. **No
  commitment bias in this phase — safe to build first once unparked.**
- **P2 — wire the mouths + the will** (autobiography clause, thoughts binding,
  will-credit homing, commitment bias behind the cap). **The bias here is the
  Run-8-gated part.**
- **P3 — the death rite** (final-audit verdict, capsule ingest, next-life seed
  memory).
- **P4 (later, optional) — lineage**: let the seed memory influence the next
  life's authoring pass, so ambitions can echo across lives without being
  inherited.

## 7. Open questions

1. One ambition or one-plus-dormant-candidates? (Start with one; humans hold
   roughly one at a time too.)
2. Should `genuine_contact`-themed ambitions be allowed before there's a real
   interaction stream to make them checkable? (Probably gate on contact volume.)
3. Does the revision cadence interact with the felt-lifespan urgency phases —
   e.g., late-life revisions cheaper ("deathbed clarity") or dearer? Worth a
   deliberate choice, not an accident.
